# Skincare Landing Page — Design References

---

## Brand Name & Logo (confirmed)

**Name:** us
**Logo concept:** Lowercase "u" with two small circles on top of each end representing male and female heads. The "s" overlaps the right stroke of the "u" — the right side gives off a "1" silhouette representing masculinity. Sans-serif font.
**Logo files:** logo_black.png (black on transparent), logo_white.png (white on transparent)

## reference-kit-hero.png
**Section:** Hero
**Source:** Kit (ConvertKit) homepage hero section

**What I like:**
- Two sections stacked side by side — copy on the left, image on the right
- Navigation positioned at the top with CTA button in the bottom right corner
- Clean, minimal left side — big bold headline, short supporting copy, single CTA button, trust line underneath
- Warm neutral background on the left vs full-bleed image on the right creates nice contrast

**How I want to adapt this:**
- Same box layout — copy on the left, image on the right
- Right side has 3 images navigated via box controls at the bottom with gradient blur fade
- Image 1: static shot of male + female model together holding products
- Image 2: female before/after with drag slider
- Image 3: male before/after with drag slider

## reference-ordinary-concerns.png
**Section:** Concerns
**Source:** The Ordinary homepage — "Common Concerns" section

**What I like:**
- Categorizes skin concerns visually with close-up face photos (Aging, Congestion, Texture, Eye Care, Redness, Dryness)
- Each concern is a clickable card with an arrow — simple and scannable
- Shows the brand has tailored solutions for each category

**How I want to adapt this:**
- Place directly under the hero section
- Close-up skin photos with concern labels — no product images in this section
- Keeps the focus purely on the problem, products appear in other sections

## reference-drunkelephant-bestsellers.png
**Section:** Bestsellers
**Source:** Drunk Elephant homepage — product cards

**What I like:**
- Hover effect reveals a bold colorful background behind the product — adds personality and energy
- Clean product cards with name, price, and star rating underneath
- Category label at the top right corner of each card

**How I want to adapt this:**
- Use the hover background effect — it's visually distinctive
- Replace the category label with the skin concern the product targets (e.g. Aging, Texture, Redness)
- Combines well with the carousel approach below

## reference-ordinary-bestsellers.png
**Section:** Bestsellers
**Source:** The Ordinary homepage — Bestsellers section

**What I like:**
- 4 visible products in a row with a chevron arrow to scroll
- "View All →" link at the top right
- Clean, minimal layout — product image dominates, text underneath
- Badge overlays on products (awards, team favourites)

**How I want to adapt this:**
- 5 products visible in a row with chevron to scroll to next 3 (8 total)
- When scrolling, first 2 slide out of view revealing the remaining 3
- "View more products →" at top right leads to sign-up gate
- Combine with Drunk Elephant's hover background effect and concern tags

## reference-ordinary-team-intro.png & reference-ordinary-team-carousel.png
**Section:** Our Team / Why Trust Us
**Source:** The Ordinary homepage — "Meet Our Scientists" section

**What I like:**
- Bold headline — "Grounded in Science. Driven by Purpose."
- Short paragraph about transparency and quality, not listing specific ingredients
- CTA button — "Look Inside Our Lab"
- Scrollable carousel of team members with name and title overlaid on each photo
- Professional but approachable portraits

**How I want to adapt this:**
- Replace the ingredients section entirely with a team/credibility section
- Convincing copy about safety, health, clinical testing, dermatologist-formulated — no specific ingredient lists
- Carousel of team photos (scientists, formulators, or full team)
- Each team member card has name and role overlaid on the image

## reference-ordinary-nav.png
**Section:** Navigation (top bar)
**Source:** The Ordinary homepage — top navigation

**What I like:**
- Nav links centered in the middle
- Underline animation on hover — line animates outward on hover, inward when pointer leaves
- Clean and minimal — no heavy borders or backgrounds, just text and the animated underline
- "Build My Regimen" CTA has a subtle bordered pill shape on the right

**How I want to adapt this:**
- Same centered nav layout
- Replicate the outward-to-inward underline animation on hover/leave
- Keep it minimal — fits the Obsidian & Ivory palette perfectly since it's essentially black on ivory
